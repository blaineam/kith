Privacy, in plain words

Your keys never leave your phone.

Here's the honest version of how Haven keeps your life yours — no asterisks, no fine print.

What stays private

  • Every post, message, photo, video and call is encrypted on your device before it's sent.
  • The secret keys that unlock your content live only on your devices — stored in the Secure Enclave on Apple devices.
  • There's no central account, no profile database, no advertising ID, no analytics.
  • Choose your own storage — your iCloud, your S3 bucket, your NAS. We never hold your keys.

The honest limits

  • A relay (an optional helper) can see that some encrypted bytes passed through, but never what they say.
  • To deliver bytes to you, a relay briefly handles your IP address — that's just how the internet works. It's never logged, and never tied to who you are.
  • Want full IP hiding? There's an opt-in onion / Tor mode for that.
  • If someone steals your unlocked, malware-infected device, they have your device. No app on earth fixes that.
The promise: never logged, never linked to you, optionally fully hidden. For the full picture, read the threat model and architecture docs.
Want it even more reliable?

Run a relay for your circle.

A relay is a tiny, always-on helper that holds your circle's encrypted messages for friends who are offline, so nothing is missed. It can never read a word — and it's free to run on an old laptop or a Raspberry Pi.

Set up a relay 
curl -fsSL https://wemiller.com/apps/haven/relay/install.sh | sh
haven-relay run --link "haven-relay://circle#…"

One command to install, one to go live. No ports, no domain, no cloud required.
